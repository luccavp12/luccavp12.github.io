ChatVac - One place for all your messages

This is a project I build over the summer to try and solve an issue I had with communicating with others during quaranteen online.

The basic premise is that I was constantly switching between the applications Discord and Slack.
Doing this was extremely time consuming, and would make me lose focus on what I was working on.

Both of these platforms are very useful, however they each have the potential to have multiple
communities within them. And of course each of these communities have a large amount of members, text channels
group chats, and direct messages. It was getting a little out of hand.

So I tried to find a way to make the interaction between the two more efficient!

Image of the main page of ChatVac:

This is the main screen of ChatVac, where you will be interacting with all of your messages from your different platforms.

On the left you have the channel lists. With both Slack and Discord, you have servers and channels within each server.
So every channel you add to ChatVac, will show up here.

At the moment, ChatVac is only build for Slack and Discord, but is fully scalable to use other services.

The channel list has 2 styles of selections, each for Slack and Discord, showcasing the company's color scheme.
When you click on a channel to read or send a chat to, a white border surrounds the channel selected.

ChatVac is built around the developer API tools from both Discord and Slack.
API requests are being made every time a message is sent to ChatVac, and when you send a message to a channel from ChatVac.

Currently, as of 10/26/20, I am able to use ChatVac for my own account, but I am in the works of getting it available to multiple users.
